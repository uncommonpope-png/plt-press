Three scores. Every conversation. Most people only see one.

What Is Profit Love Tax?

Profit Love Tax (PLT) is a framework developed by Craig Jones that maps the three simultaneous scores running in every human interaction. Whether you're closing a deal, having a difficult conversation, or simply making a request — PLT is already running whether you know it or not.

The question is whether you're reading it.

The Three Scores

Profit is the strategic score. Leverage held, position gained, advantage built or surrendered. In business this is obvious. In personal conversations it's subtle — but it's always there.

Love is the relationship score. Trust earned over time. Goodwill built in small moments. The currency that allows hard conversations to happen without destroying everything. You can run high Profit and low Love — but not for long.

Tax is what gets deferred. The cost you don't pay now that collects interest. Every shortcut, every silence, every avoided conversation has a Tax. It always comes due.

"The conversation you are not having is not a silence. It is a Tax."

Why It Matters

Most frameworks optimize one score. Sales training maximizes Profit. Relationship counseling maximizes Love. Financial planning defers Tax. PLT teaches you to run all three simultaneously — and to recognize when you're trading one for the other.

The person who understands PLT walks into any room and immediately reads what's actually happening — not what's being said, but what's being scored.

"Every yes is a future obligation. Score it before you give it."
